Overview of yadgir, karnataka

Yadgir district is located in the state of Karnataka, India. It has a population of about 1.15 million based on the 2011 census data.

Key Facts

  • It has 6 administrative blocks - Hunsagi, Shorapur, Shahapur, Shahpur, Yadgir and Wadgera
  • The district spans over about 195,360 hectares of land
  • It falls under the Krishna Basin region and receives an average annual rainfall of about 601 mm
  • The main rivers are the Krishna and the Bhima
  • Agriculture and allied activities are the main occupation, with major crops being cotton, wheat and millets
  • As per the 2011 census, literacy rate is about 60% overall, with 70% for males and 50% for females
  • Total households in the district are about 0.25 million according to 2011 data

Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM)

  • Goal is to provide tapped water supply to all households by 2024 under the mission
  • As of early 2023, the district had achieved only 28.3% of this target, covering about 69,000 households so far
  • There is a significant gap between coverage of rural households (26%) compared to urban ones (95%)
  • Issues with quantity, quality and consistency of water supply through the installed taps
  • More testing, compliance and water source sustainability efforts needed under JJM
